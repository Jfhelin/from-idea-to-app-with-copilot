# Step 2 — Try It

**Now it is your turn. You are going to ask Copilot to build an app for you.**

You do not need to come up with an idea from scratch. Pick one from the list below, copy the prompt next to it, and paste it into Copilot Chat. That is all you need to do to get started.

---

## Fast Start (Do This First)

1. Open Copilot Chat
2. Copy ONE of the prompts below
3. Paste it and press Enter

👉 Do this now — you can read the rest later.

---

## How This Works

1. Open Copilot Chat (your facilitator will show you where this is if you are not sure)
2. Pick one of the three app ideas below
3. Copy the prompt exactly as written
4. Paste it into the chat and press Enter
5. Watch Copilot build the app
6. When it is done, open the result in your browser

---

## Choose One App Idea

Pick whichever one feels most useful or interesting to you. You only need to choose one.

---

### Option A — KPI Dashboard

A simple one-page view of key business numbers for a sales team.

**Copy and paste this prompt:**

> Build me a simple KPI dashboard for a sales team. It should show four headline numbers at the top: total revenue this quarter, number of deals closed, average deal size, and customer satisfaction score. Below that, show a table of the top five customers with their name, revenue, and status. Use realistic-looking sample numbers. Make the layout clean and easy to read. The whole thing should open in a browser with no setup required.

---

### Option B — Customer Overview

A searchable list of customers with a detail panel for each one.

**Copy and paste this prompt:**

> Build me a customer overview tool. It should show a list of ten customers on the left side. Each customer entry should show their name, company, and account status. When I click on a customer, the right side should show their details: contact information, account value, last activity date, and a short note. Use realistic-looking sample data. Make it simple and clean. The whole thing should open in a browser with no setup required.

---

### Option C — Task Tracker

A simple board for tracking tasks across three columns: To Do, In Progress, and Done.

**Copy and paste this prompt:**

> Build me a simple task tracker with three columns: To Do, In Progress, and Done. Each column should show task cards. Each card should have a task title, an owner name, and a priority level (High, Medium, or Low). Pre-populate it with about twelve realistic-looking sample tasks spread across the three columns. Make it clean and easy to read. The whole thing should open in a browser with no setup required.

---

## While Copilot Is Building

You do not need to do anything while Copilot works. Give it a moment.

When it finishes, it will tell you what it built and how to open it. 

## Opening Your App

When Copilot finishes:

- Look for a message that says how to open the app
- Usually this means opening a file called `index.html`

If you are unsure:

👉 Ask Copilot:
> "How do I open the app?"

Or ask your facilitator.

---

## What to Look For Once It Opens

When the app is open in your browser, check:

- Does it show real-looking content — names, numbers, and labels?
- Does the layout make sense for the idea you chose?
- Is anything missing or confusing?

You do not need it to be perfect. You just need something to react to.

---

## If Something Looks Wrong

Do not worry. That is what the next step is for.

If Copilot said it was done but the app is not opening, tell it:

> "The app is not opening. Can you make it work by just opening a file in the browser?"

If the content looks like placeholder text (for example, "Lorem ipsum" or "Item 1, Item 2"), tell it:

> "The sample data looks like placeholder text. Please replace it with realistic-looking names and numbers."
